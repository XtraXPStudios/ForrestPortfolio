Communication Poster

While attending Multimedia Production at Lethbridge Polytechnic, we were tasked with designing live posters that reflect the Core Competency that we were assigned.

I was assigned Communication! My focus was on trying to find a unique way to portray the importance of communication, which led me to interspecies communication. If different species can work together and communicate for survival, why does humanity struggle to communicate?!

Motion Graphics

While attending Multimedia Production, one of our assignments was a Motion Graphics video using multiple text graphics and effects that tells you a little more about us!

My challenge with this assignment was Adobe After Effects. AE does not like exporting multiple text effects! In the end, to get around the issue, I had to break up the video into 3 sections, export each section separately, and then bring them into Adobe Premiere Pro to combine them and export them as one video.

This assignment was definitely a learning curve with Adobe programs!

Nobyl of Chernobyl

While attending Multimedia Production our final assignment in 3D Modeling was to create a 3D character and then create an animation inside Blender.

This is my character Nobyl of Chernobyl! Inspired by the mushroom discovered in Chernobyl that was absorbing the radiation. I made a bit of a twist on his mushroom head and the inspiration for that was from the zombies in The Last of Us.

Pennywise Floaties

While attending Multimedia Production, one of our assignments for Video Graphics class was to create an ad or commercial! We had the creative freedom to choose what type we wanted to make and I decided to create an animation commercial using Procreate on my iPad.

The inspiration was clearly my love of horror movies! I combined both It (my favourite horror movie) and then I took the cereal idea from Terrifier 2 (also a favourite series for me). The idea is to have multiple cereal brands with horror movie themes!

This animation was and still is my favourite! My plan is to make more fun little commercials to make this into a series of sorts!
