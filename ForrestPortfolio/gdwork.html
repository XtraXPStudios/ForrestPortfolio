JurassicFun

While attending Multimedia Production, one of our first branding assignments was to create different types of merch. For this one in particular, we had to create a sticker of some kind, so I combined my love for sharks, bats, and Jurassic Park and combined them into one image! I wanted to create something that both screamed Millenial and nerd at the same time, so it felt very me!

Graphic Mee

Learning programs like Adobe Illustrator and Affinity reignited my love for art. I had gone through quite a rut before attending Multimedia Production. With the guidance and support of the amazing instructors, I found that spark again.

This piece was a small passion project in between assignments. It was meant to be a Self-Portrait and the style was inspired from the "Life is Strange" art.

Trading Cards

While attending Multimedia Production, one of our final assignments in year 1 was to create our own trading cards. I had decided very early that I wanted to make the instructors of MMP into superhero cards! So, "The Heros of MMP" were born!

This assignment was a lot of fun for me, taking the instructors and creating their superhero persona's was a fun challenge! I had made both hero's and "Villans" but even my villans don't necessarily stay evil! All have the potential to choose good!

Communication Poster

While attending Multimedia Production at Lethbridge Polytechnic, we were tasked with designing live posters that reflect the Core Competency that we were assigned. These are the stills from that animation.

I was assigned Communication! My focus was on trying to find a unique way to portray the importance of communication, which led me to interspecies communication. If different species can work together and communicate for survival, why does humanity struggle to communicate?!

Nobyl of Chernobyl

This is my character Nobyl of Chernobyl! Inspired by the mushroom discovered in Chernobyl that was absorbing the radiation. I made a bit of a twist on his mushroom head and the inspiration for that was from the zombies in The Last of Us.

This project was also during Multimedia Production and it was our first experience 3D modeling our very own characters! This assignment was also a huge learning opportunity and I thoroughly enjoyed learning the similarities and differences between Blender and Adobe After Effects when it comes to rendering animations!

This drawing was my reference sketch for the final 3D model of Nobyl that I later animated inside Blender!

Capstone Project

Capstone was the big passion project we were assigned in our final semester of Multimedia Production. It was a unique assignment where we chose what we wanted to create and spent the entire semester executing our vision! Though it was a passion project, a part of the assignment was to have learning goals that you wanted to reach by the end of the project! It was a wonderful way to grow your skillset for the career you wanted when you completed the program.

My Capstone Project was a 3D level in a video game that shows the mechanics and the vision for what the game would be once completed. My learning goals were to focus on interior staging of an environment (school floor), storytelling/hook-writing, and to create certain 3D assets in Blender myself; the main character, the monster, and the plant that grows in the environment.

The end goal for me is to have this video game be the first one I develop with my company, XtraXP Studios! These are the sketches and designs for the assets I created for the project!
